Whose Turn - Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 19, 2026

Overview

Whose Turn is designed with your privacy in mind. The app stores all data locally on your device and does not collect, transmit, or share any personal information.

Data Storage

All data you enter into Whose Turn — including friend names, photos, and transaction history — is stored exclusively on your device using Apple's on-device storage. No data ever leaves your device.

Photos

If you choose to add a photo for a friend, the photo is stored locally on your device as part of the app's data. Whose Turn does not access your full photo library — you select individual photos through Apple's system photo picker, and only the photos you explicitly choose are saved within the app.

No Analytics or Tracking

Whose Turn does not use any analytics services, advertising frameworks, or tracking technologies. There are no third-party SDKs in the app.

No Account Required

Whose Turn does not require you to create an account, sign in, or provide any personal information to use the app.

No Network Access

Whose Turn does not connect to the internet. The app functions entirely offline.

Data Deletion

You can delete any data at any time by removing friends or transactions within the app. Uninstalling the app will permanently delete all associated data from your device.

Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, you can reach us at whoseturn@jeffkeltner.com.
